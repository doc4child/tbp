Privacy Policy

Teddy Bear Pediatrics PLLC ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website, use our services, or otherwise engage with us. Please read this policy carefully. If you do not agree with the terms, please do not use our services.

1. Information We Collect

  • Personal Information: Name, date of birth, contact details, insurance information, and other identifying details provided during registration or communication with us.
  • Health Information: Medical history, treatment records, prescriptions, and other protected health information (PHI) necessary for providing care.
  • Technical Information: IP addresses, browser type, and usage data collected when you visit our website.
  • Payment Information: Billing details, credit/debit card information, and transaction history for payment processing.

2. How We Use Your Information

  • Provide pediatric healthcare services
  • Schedule and manage appointments
  • Process billing and insurance claims
  • Communicate with patients and guardians regarding medical care
  • Improve our website and services
  • Ensure compliance with legal and regulatory requirements

3. Sharing Your Information

We do not sell or rent your personal information. However, we may share your information with:

  • Healthcare providers involved in your treatment
  • Insurance companies for claim processing
  • Third-party service providers assisting with operations (e.g., billing, IT support)
  • Legal authorities when required by law or for safety reasons

4. Data Protection and Security

We implement security measures to protect your personal and health information from unauthorized access, disclosure, or misuse. However, no data transmission over the internet is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

5. Your Rights and Choices

  • Access and obtain a copy of your health records
  • Request corrections to inaccurate information
  • Restrict or object to certain data processing
  • Withdraw consent for non-essential data collection
  • File a complaint if you believe your privacy rights have been violated

6. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Our website may use cookies and similar tracking technologies to improve user experience and analyze website performance. You can manage cookie preferences through your browser settings.

7. Third-Party Links

Our website may contain links to third-party websites. We are not responsible for their privacy practices and encourage you to review their policies.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with the updated effective date.
